## 1. one-box — min 8s

A workspace is one box: your workflows, tables, knowledge bases, and files all live inside it together.

## 2. members-join — min 10s

You don't share the objects one by one — you add people to the workspace, each with a permission level. Joining the box gives access to everything inside it.

## 3. the-boundary — min 10s

The box is also a boundary. When a workflow in another workspace reaches for our table, the request stops at the edge — resources don't cross workspaces.

## 4. connect-an-account — min 12s

Accounts connect to the box the same way. You hand the workspace a Google credential once, and Gmail, Drive, and Calendar become available to everyone inside.

## 5. one-credential-three-blocks — min 14s

Inside a workflow, one Credential block feeds all three of these blocks — every reference resolves to the same account. If you change the account in that one place, every block that references it follows.

## 6. the-unit — min 8s

So the workspace is the unit: objects, members, and credentials, in one box a team shares.
